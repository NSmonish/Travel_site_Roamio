Things To Do In New York

Must-Visit Places

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, a symbol of freedom and democracy, is an iconic landmark in New York Harbor. Take a ferry to Liberty Island, explore the museum exhibits, and climb to the crown for panoramic views of the city skyline and harbor.

Times Square

Times Square, also known as "The Crossroads of the World," is a vibrant entertainment hub in the heart of Manhattan. Experience the dazzling lights, towering billboards, and bustling crowds as you explore the theaters, shops, and attractions of this iconic destination.

Central Park

Central Park is a sprawling urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan, offering a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. Explore its scenic paths, picturesque bridges, and tranquil lakes, and visit iconic landmarks like Bethesda Terrace and the Central Park Zoo.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building, an architectural marvel and symbol of New York City, offers breathtaking views from its observation decks. Ascend to the 86th and 102nd floors for panoramic vistas of the city skyline, including landmarks like the Chrysler Building and Statue of Liberty.

Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge, a historic suspension bridge spanning the East River, offers stunning views of the Manhattan skyline and New York Harbor. Walk, bike, or drive across the bridge, and admire its Gothic architecture, granite towers, and intricate cable patterns.

Rockefeller Center

Rockefeller Center is a historic complex of commercial buildings, entertainment venues, and public art in Midtown Manhattan. Visit the Top of the Rock observation deck for panoramic views, skate at the iconic ice rink, and attend seasonal events like the Christmas Tree Lighting Ceremony.

Broadway

Broadway is the epicenter of live theater and musical entertainment in New York City. Catch a world-class Broadway show, ranging from classic musicals to cutting-edge dramas, at one of the iconic theaters in the Theater District.

Wall Street

Wall Street, located in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan, is synonymous with global finance and commerce. Visit iconic landmarks like the New York Stock Exchange and Federal Hall, explore historic cobblestone streets, and learn about the history of American capitalism.

Museum Mile

Museum Mile, along Fifth Avenue on the Upper East Side, is home to some of the world's most renowned museums and cultural institutions. Explore the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Guggenheim Museum, and the Museum of Modern Art, among others, and immerse yourself in art, history, and culture.

Adventures

Go ice skating at Wollman Rink in Central Park, a beloved winter tradition for locals and visitors alike. Glide across the ice, surrounded by the park's scenic beauty and iconic skyline views, and warm up with hot cocoa at the rinkside café.

Explore New York City by bike with a Citi Bike rental, offering convenient access to bike stations throughout the city. Pedal along dedicated bike lanes, explore scenic waterfront paths, and discover hidden gems in neighborhoods like Brooklyn and Queens.

Take a sunset cruise along the Hudson River with Circle Line Sightseeing Cruises, offering stunning views of the Manhattan skyline and iconic landmarks like the Statue of Liberty and One World Trade Center. Enjoy live narration, refreshments, and photo opportunities on this unforgettable boat tour.
